My Hometown Surabaya

I have been living in Surabaya since now. Surabaya is the capital city of East Java Province, Indonesia, as well as the largest metropolitan city in the province. Surabaya is the second largest city in Indonesia after Jakarta. The city is located 800 km east of Jakarta, or 435 km northwest of Denpasar, Bali. Surabaya is located on the north coast of the eastern part of Java Island and is opposite the Madura Strait and the Java Sea. Surabaya is known as the City of Heroes because of its history which is very calculated in the struggle of Arek-Arek Suroboyo (Surabaya youths) in defending the independence of the Indonesian nation from colonial attacks. Surabaya was also the largest city in the Dutch East Indies and became a commercial center in the archipelago which was parallel to Hong Kong and Shanghai at that time. Unair

The establishment of Airlangga University has a long history. Before Unair was officially established, on 9 and 11 October 1847, a proposal was submitted to the Dutch Colonial Government to educate talented Javanese youths to become experts in health practice. On January 2, 1849, through Government Decree no. 22, established NIAS (Nederlandsch Indische Artsen School)[12] as a place for medical education in Surabaya. Since 1913, medical education in Surabaya has taken place at Jalan Kedungdoro 38 Surabaya. In 1923, the NIAS building was moved from Jalan Kedungdoro to where the Faculty of Medicine was founded on Jalan Mayjen. Prof. Dr. Moestopo, Surabaya.
